### H2: At the Accident Scene

#### H3: Step 1 – Check for Injuries
- Assess yourself and passengers
- Call 911 immediately if injuries
- Don't move seriously injured persons
- Prioritize safety over everything

#### H3: Step 2 – Move to Safety
- If possible, move vehicles out of traffic
- Turn on hazard lights
- Set up flares or triangles if available
- Stay away from traffic lanes

#### H3: Step 3 – Call 911
- Always report accidents with injuries
- Texas law requires reporting certain accidents
- Police report crucial for your claim
- Request medical assistance if needed

#### H3: Step 4 – Exchange Information
- Name and contact information
- Driver's license numbers
- Insurance company and policy numbers
- Vehicle make, model, license plate
- Don't discuss fault

#### H3: Step 5 – Document Everything
- Take photos of all vehicles
- Photograph the accident scene
- Capture road conditions, signs, signals
- Document visible injuries
- Get wide shots and close-ups

#### H3: Step 6 – Get Witness Information
- Names and phone numbers
- Ask what they saw
- Their contact info may be crucial

---

### H2: What NOT to Do at the Scene

#### H3: Don't Admit Fault
- Even saying "I'm sorry" can hurt
- Fault is determined by investigation
- Let insurance and police assess

#### H3: Don't Argue with Other Driver
- Stay calm and factual
- Exchange information only
- Don't escalate confrontation

#### H3: Don't Leave the Scene
- Hit and run is a crime in Texas
- Stay until police release you
- Even for minor accidents

#### H3: Don't Refuse Medical Attention
- Adrenaline masks pain
- Some injuries appear later
- Refusal documented against you

---

### H2: After Leaving the Scene

#### H3: Seek Medical Attention
- Go to ER if serious injuries
- Visit urgent care or doctor soon
- Document all injuries
- Follow all treatment recommendations

#### H3: Report to Your Insurance
- Notify your insurer promptly
- Provide basic facts only
- Don't give recorded statement yet

#### H3: Don't Post on Social Media
- Insurance monitors social media
- Posts can be used against you
- Avoid discussing accident online

### H2: Dealing with Insurance Companies

#### H3: Their Goal vs. Your Goal
- They want to minimize payout
- You deserve full compensation
- Don't let them take advantage

#### H3: Don't Give Recorded Statements
- You're not required to
- Statements used against you
- Let your lawyer handle communications

#### H3: Don't Accept Quick Settlements
- Early offers usually lowball
- You may not know full injury extent
- Signing releases ends your claim

### H2: When You Need Emergency Care

#### H3: Signs of Serious Injury
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe headache
- Neck or back pain
- Numbness or tingling
- Abdominal pain
- Difficulty breathing

### H2: Texas-Specific Considerations

#### H3: Texas is an At-Fault State
- Negligent driver responsible
- Can file claim against their insurance
- Comparative negligence rules

#### H3: Minimum Insurance Requirements
- $30,000 bodily injury per person
- $60,000 bodily injury per accident
- $25,000 property damage
- Many drivers carry minimums only

#### H3: Statute of Limitations
- 2 years to file lawsuit
- Don't wait to take action
