### H2: Punitive Damages (Exemplary Damages)

#### H3: What Are Punitive Damages?
- Meant to punish defendant
- Deter future misconduct
- Not compensation, but punishment

#### H3: When Punitive Damages Apply
- Gross negligence
- Intentional misconduct
- Fraud or malice
- Reckless disregard for safety

#### H3: Punitive Damage Caps in Texas
- Greater of: $200,000 OR
- 2× economic damages + equal non-economic (up to $750,000)
- Exceptions for certain crimes

---

### H2: Damage Caps in Texas

#### H3: Personal Injury Cases
- No cap on economic damages
- No cap on non-economic damages (except med mal)
- Punitive damages capped

#### H3: Medical Malpractice Caps
- $250,000 per physician
- $250,000-$500,000 per hospital
- Economic damages uncapped
- Link to: `/medical-malpractice-lawyer/`

#### H3: Government Entity Caps
- Texas Tort Claims Act limits
- $250,000 per person
- $500,000 per occurrence

### H2: How Damages Are Calculated

#### H3: Economic Damages Calculation
- Add up all documented expenses
- Project future costs
- Economic experts for lost earnings

#### H3: Non-Economic Damages Methods
- Multiplier method (1.5-5× medical bills)
- Per diem method (daily rate × days)
- Jury determination at trial

#### H3: Role of Expert Witnesses
- Medical experts for future care
- Economists for lost earnings
- Vocational experts for career impact
