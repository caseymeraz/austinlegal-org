### H2: Questions to Ask During Consultation

#### H3: About Their Experience
- How long have you practiced personal injury law?
- How many cases like mine have you handled?
- What were the outcomes?

#### H3: About Your Case
- What do you think my case is worth?
- What are the strengths and weaknesses?
- How long will it take?

#### H3: About Their Process
- Who will actually work on my case?
- How often will you update me?
- How do you communicate (phone, email)?

#### H3: About Fees
- What is your fee percentage?
- Are there any costs I might owe if we lose?
- What expenses are deducted from settlement?

---

### H2: Understanding Fee Structures

#### H3: Contingency Fees Explained
- No upfront cost
- Lawyer paid from recovery
- Typical percentage: 33-40%
- Align lawyer's interests with yours

#### H3: Fee Percentage Variations
- Pre-lawsuit vs. post-lawsuit filing
- Trial vs. settlement rates
- Sliding scales

#### H3: Costs vs. Fees
- Costs: expenses like filing fees, experts
- Fees: attorney's payment
- Who pays costs if you lose?

#### H3: Reading the Fee Agreement
- Understand before you sign
- Ask questions about unclear terms
- Get everything in writing

### H2: Red Flags to Avoid

#### H3: Guarantees of Specific Results
- No lawyer can guarantee outcomes
- Promises are red flags

#### H3: Pressure to Sign Immediately
- Good lawyers give you time
- High-pressure tactics = bad sign

#### H3: Poor Communication
- Doesn't return calls
- Difficult to reach
- Impatient with questions

#### H3: Lack of Trial Experience
- Never been to trial
- Insurance companies know this
- Affects settlement leverage

#### H3: Handling Too Many Cases
- "Case mill" concerns
- Your case needs attention
- Ask about caseload

#### H3: No Clear Fee Agreement
- Won't provide written agreement
- Vague about costs
- Hidden fees
