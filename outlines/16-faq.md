### H2: Insurance Questions

#### H3: Should I talk to the other driver's insurance company?
- Generally, no
- They're not on your side
- Let your lawyer handle communications

#### H3: What if the insurance company offers a quick settlement?
- Usually a lowball offer
- Don't accept without evaluation
- You may be signing away rights

#### H3: What if the at-fault driver has no insurance?
- Uninsured motorist (UM) coverage
- Your own policy options
- Other possible sources

#### H3: Will my insurance rates go up if I file a claim?
- Not usually if not at fault
- Texas law protections
- Depends on your policy

### H2: Medical Treatment Questions

#### H3: Should I see a doctor even if I feel fine?
- Yes, absolutely
- Some injuries appear later
- Documentation is crucial

#### H3: Who pays for my medical bills during my case?
- Your health insurance initially
- Settlement will reimburse
- Medical liens possible

#### H3: Can I choose my own doctor?
- Yes, in personal injury cases
- Workers' comp may have restrictions
- Choose experienced injury doctors

#### H3: What if I had a pre-existing condition?
- You can still recover
- "Eggshell plaintiff" rule
- Insurance will argue, but law is on your side

### H2: Compensation Questions

#### H3: What damages can I recover in Texas?
- Economic damages (medical, lost wages)
- Non-economic damages (pain, suffering)
- Punitive damages (rare cases)
- Link to: `/types-of-damages-texas/`

#### H3: Is there a cap on pain and suffering in Texas?
- No cap for most injury cases
- Medical malpractice has caps
- Government claims have caps

#### H3: How is pain and suffering calculated?
- Multiplier method
- Per diem method
- Jury discretion at trial

#### H3: Can I recover if I was partially at fault?
- Yes, if less than 51% at fault
- Recovery reduced by your percentage
- Texas comparative negligence rule
