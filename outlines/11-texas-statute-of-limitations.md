### H2: The General Rule: 2 Years

#### H3: Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code § 16.003
- Standard 2-year limitation period
- Starts from date of injury/accident
- Applies to most personal injury claims

#### H3: What Happens When You Miss the Deadline
- Case dismissed
- Cannot pursue compensation
- Rare exceptions

### H2: Exceptions That Extend the Deadline

#### H3: Discovery Rule
- When injury not immediately apparent
- Clock starts when injury discovered
- Common in medical malpractice

#### H3: Minor Children
- Deadline tolled until age 18
- Then 2 years to file
- Parent can file during minority

#### H3: Mental Incapacity
- Tolled during incapacitation
- Must be legally incapacitated

#### H3: Defendant Leaves Texas
- Time defendant absent doesn't count
- Rarely applicable today

---

### H2: Exceptions That Shorten the Deadline

#### H3: Government Entities
- Claims against cities, counties, state
- Must file notice within 6 months
- Shorter deadlines apply

#### H3: Texas Tort Claims Act
- Sovereign immunity limitations
- Strict notice requirements
- Damage caps

---

### H2: Statute of Repose vs. Statute of Limitations

#### H3: What is a Statute of Repose?
- Absolute cutoff regardless of discovery
- Applies to certain claim types

#### H3: Medical Malpractice: 10 Years
- No claim after 10 years from act
- Even if injury discovered later

#### H3: Product Liability: 15 Years
- No claim 15 years after sale
- Exceptions for latent injuries
