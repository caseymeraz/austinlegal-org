Understanding Personal Injury Law in Texas

Texas personal injury law allows accident victims to seek compensation when someone else's negligence causes them harm. To successfully pursue a personal injury claim in Texas, you must establish four key elements: the defendant owed you a duty of care, they breached that duty, their breach caused your injuries, and you suffered actual damages as a result.

Statute of Limitations

In Texas, you generally have two years from the date of your injury to file a personal injury lawsuit. Missing this deadline typically bars you from recovering any compensation, regardless of how strong your case may be.

Learn about filing deadlines →

Comparative Negligence

Texas follows a modified comparative fault rule. You can recover damages as long as you're less than 51% at fault for the accident. Your compensation is reduced by your percentage of fault.

Learn about Texas damage rules →

Types of Damages

Texas allows recovery of economic damages (medical bills, lost wages), non-economic damages (pain and suffering), and in rare cases, punitive damages for gross negligence.
